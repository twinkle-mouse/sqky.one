# Intro

Hi! This is Stella! 🩷 *squeak squeak*

I’ve written this writer’s note after some battling with myself. This story is a proud one for me, and its reception by the people I’ve shown it to have surprised my confidence greatly.

This is the first actual story I’ve written with the intention to publish. I’ve had scraps in random places before, but never had any real intentions of ever publishing them. I don’t come from a writing background, *I never even graduated highschool*— I mean, I don’t even speak English natively— so I have some self-esteem problems with regards to my English writing skills.

I only really started trying to write seriously after encouragement from some friends, and I’m glad I did. I still don’t know if what I’ve written really were anything good, but, I’ll have to take my friends’ words for it… for now.

So, if you’ve read the story and you’re now reading this note: **thank you for reading**! I’m always welcome to criticism (positive or negative), so if you have any, please send it my way.

For the rest of this note, I’ve felt an autistic need to explain the lil references I’ve made in the story. I hope these interest you.

# References

## The Title

“*In the absence of comfort*” is a reference to the song in the same title by *Vylet Pony*. The song has nothing to do with the story, but the name of the song has stuck around in my head ever since I listened to the song at 3 AM one night. *Vylet Pony*‘s music has been a staple of my journey with mental health and with artistic endeavours, and the idea for the story struck me one day in the form of the melancholy-inducing phrase, as a tonal inspiration.

## Hijo de la luna

In *The Burn*, there’s an excerpt in Spanish:

> “*Luna quieres ser madre, y no encuentras querer que te haga mujer.*”
> “*Dime luna de plata, ¿qué pretendes hacer con un niño de piel?*”

This is a reference to a popular song by *Mecano* called *[Hijo de la luna](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iM9Nx7RYPqs)*. This song actually goes back far into my childhood for me.

I was like, 10 maybe? Doing my homework in the living room, listening to *<abbr>MLP</abbr>* (My Little Pony) fan songs as I was into the show at the time.

Then, a *Mandarin* translation of a [<abbr>MLP</abbr> fan animation](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6WXMHZS-KP4) of the song *Hijo de la luna* popped into my Youtube recommendations and I started listening. And I read about the story portrayed in the song from the description, and I just kept listening on repeat.

<small>I unfortunately can’t find the translation video anymore.</small>

---

When I wrote the story, I was already living in *Spain*. While working on *The Chill*, I suddenly remembered the song from my childhood, after a decade of it not crossing my mind.
I listened to it again—and it was just as I remembered it—and I decided that I should add a lil reference to the song, somewhere in the story.
And the perfect spot presented itself when I was finishing off *The Burn*—for it to space out the emotional scene in the forest.
The vibes fit, the lyrics fit, and more importantly, it held a link to my childhood, which was one of the main things that the story was tugging on—a vague, if even existent, feeling of homeliness in one’s own memory of growing up, and if you even belonged in that memory.

Also, *yes*, I know *Lunette* is a French diminutive: that’s why the final part refers to “la Lunette” and “la Lune”. Partially, I chose a French name because… this is a story in English and we have French names here,
but also partially I just didn’t know how Spanish makes diminutives yet.

## Elle

*Elle* is actually a reference to someone very close to me once before. Her display name was “*Wolfy Elle*.”

She(or well, her headmate at the time) helped me with accepting my identity as a mouse, so I made a tribute to her in name. :3

# Final Words

This is a story I still feel proud of after all these months. I’ve been working on a new story titled “*An Inverted Paracosm*,” but I still keep going back to this story emotionally.

“*In the absence of comfort*” was the first thing I’ve really ever put effort into writing, it contains a snapshot of my emotions, my struggles, my aspirations and my yearnings in that specific span of my time on this earth.
I hope to always be able to go back to this story, in its soft tone and embracing touch, with whatever story I may write in the future.

“*An Inverted Paracosm*” has been brewing in the background for a while now, and I’ve written quite a lot of words for it now. It’s a much more sombre story, it’s about *mental health*, but I still hope to have nuggets of the kind of *comforting light* that “*In the absence of comfort*” brings me, and a lot of people who’ve read it.
Although it’s going to be a sad story, I still hope to bring hope through my words, because life is always full of hope and kindness, even when you’ve hit rock bottom.

Thank you for reading this note, and have a *squeaky* day. 🩷
